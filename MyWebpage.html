Fibrant colors show you the way to antioxydants

The colors in fruits and vegetables are often a sign that they contain antioxidants that can be very powerful and often the more colorful variant has more of the green stuff. E.g. red onions have more antioxidants than white onions (76% more) and red cabbage eight times more than white!

Dark green leafy vegetables

Dark green leafy vegetables are a good source of nutrients and Havard researches could show that greens provide protection against major chronic diseases: For every added daily serving the risk for heart attacks and stroke is reduced by 20%.

Sources: Michael Gregor: How not to die
